UX DESIGN

Successful products and services solve real end-user problems. Our user-centered design mindset enables us to discover true user needs.

Using evidence gathered from quantitative and qualitative research, we can make well informed design decisions at every step of the development process.

User tasks are mapped and analysed so as to optimise navigation and interactions. Information architecture drives content hierarchy and structure prioritisation according to what users need to achieve. The iterative application of UX research, wireframes and prototypes, as practiced by us in Scrum, validate designs and improve features as early as needed (but not earlier). The final graphic design ensures visual consistency across all platforms and features.

SCRUM

We use Scrum to improve software, products and companies, whether bringing order to chaos in younger organisations or focusing on effective value generation in established ones.

In both cases the outcome is disciplined and sustainable delivery of valuable software and products. Scrum’s focus on regular delivery gives organisations feedback loops and evidence from both market and internal stakeholders.

This evidence is used to reflect, learn, adapt and improve. Scrum traces its roots to the Toyota Production System, thus human centric and focused on continuous improvement. As such, it blends well with software evolution and UX design, which in turn work better inside the iterative nature of the Scrum framework.

LEAN STARTUP

Solving the wrong problem is extremely expensive, if not catastrophic. Requirements are typically assumptions that need to be validated, preferably before huge investments are made.

In our approach, an MVP is the shortest possible product backlog maximised for learning and de-risking projects. The Lean Startup approach gives us the framework to constantly validate and prioritise requirements based on value, thus ensuring a fit between the software produced and the needs of its future users.

A natural fit for Scrum backlog grooming and planning. We combine Lean Startup and UX design to make certain we always solve the right problem and that we always pay attention to our solution’s stakeholders.
